Historical Monuments

Red Fort (Lal Qila)

UNESCO World Heritage Site, historic fort with Mughal architecture.

Qutub Minar

UNESCO World Heritage Site, tallest minaret in India.

Humayun's Tomb

UNESCO World Heritage Site, Mughal-era tomb with gardens.

India Gate

War memorial arch honoring Indian soldiers.

Jama Masjid

Largest mosque in India, with stunning architecture.

Religious Sites

Lotus Temple

Bahá'í House of Worship known for its lotus-shaped architecture.

Akshardham Temple

Hindu temple complex with exhibitions and cultural shows.

Museums and Art Galleries

National Museum

Largest museum in India, showcasing art, archaeology, and history.

National Gallery of Modern Art

Art museum featuring modern and contemporary Indian art.

Gandhi Smriti

Museum dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi, located at his former residence.

Markets and Shopping

Chandni Chowk

Historic market street known for its bustling atmosphere and street food.

Connaught Place

Commercial and financial hub with shops, restaurants, and colonial architecture.

Dilli Haat

Cultural bazaar offering handicrafts, cuisine, and performances from across India.

Gardens and Parks

Lodhi Garden

Historic park with tombs, gardens, and jogging trails.

Hauz Khas Complex

Historic village complex with medieval ruins and a deer park.

Garden of Five Senses

Botanical garden with sculptures and themed gardens.

Cultural Centers

Dilli Haat

Cultural center with crafts, music, and dance performances.

Nehru Planetarium

Astronomy center with shows and exhibits on space science.

Other Attractions

Rajpath

Historic boulevard lined with government buildings and ceremonial events.

India Gate

War memorial dedicated to soldiers who died in World War I.

Jantar Mantar

Observatory with astronomical instruments from the 18th century.
