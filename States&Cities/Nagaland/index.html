Kohima

War Cemetery

Commemorates soldiers of the World War II Battle of Kohima.

Kohima State Museum

Exhibits on Naga tribal history, culture, and artifacts.

Dzükou Valley

Famous for its trekking trails and seasonal flowers.

Kohima Zoo

Wildlife sanctuary showcasing indigenous flora and fauna.

Dimapur

Kachari Ruins

Ancient ruins of the Kachari kingdom.

Rangapahar Reserve Forest

Wildlife sanctuary with diverse bird species.

Mokokchung

Longkhum

Scenic village offering panoramic views of the surroundings.

Changtongya

Known for its orange orchards and natural beauty.

Tuensang

Chilise

Hilltop offering views of the Tuensang town.

Lungwa Village

Known for its remote location and traditional tribal culture.

Mon

Longwa Village

Traditional Konyak Naga village with a unique geographical location straddling the India-Myanmar border.

Veda Peak

Highest peak in Nagaland, offering panoramic views.

Wokha

Doyang River

Famous for the annual Amur Falcon migration.

Mount Tiyi

Sacred mountain of the Lotha Naga tribe.

Pfutsero

Pfutsero Town

Known for its pleasant climate and scenic beauty.

Pfutsero Peak

Trekking destination offering panoramic views.

Other Attractions

Satoi Range

Biodiversity hotspot with diverse flora and fauna.

Shilloi Lake

Natural lake surrounded by picturesque landscapes.

Festivals and Cultural Events

Hornbill Festival

Celebrates Naga tribal culture with traditional dance, music, and crafts.

Moatsu Festival

Ao Naga festival marking the completion of sowing season with feasting and traditional rituals.

Adventure and Nature Activities

Trekking and Hiking

Numerous trails in the hills and valleys of Nagaland.

Birdwatching

Opportunities to spot unique bird species, especially during the Amur Falcon migration season.
