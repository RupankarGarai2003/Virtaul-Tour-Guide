Top Famous Tourist Places in South Delhi

South Delhi is a hub of history, culture, and modern attractions. From ancient monuments to beautiful parks, it offers a perfect blend of the past and the present.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is one of the tallest brick minarets in the world, standing at 73 meters. Built in 1193, it symbolizes the beginning of Muslim rule in India and is surrounded by several other historic monuments.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, a Bahá'í House of Worship, is famed for its flowerlike shape. Open to people of all religions, it is one of the most visited buildings in the world and offers a serene environment for meditation and reflection.

Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb is the first garden tomb built in India, commissioned by the widow of Emperor Humayun in 1570. This UNESCO World Heritage site is known for its grand Persian-style architecture and beautiful gardens.

Hauz Khas Village

Hauz Khas Village is a trendy urban village in South Delhi known for its historical Hauz Khas Complex, which dates back to the medieval period. The area is now filled with cafes, art galleries, and boutiques, blending history with contemporary culture.

Lodi Gardens

Lodi Gardens is a sprawling park in South Delhi that houses the tombs of several rulers from the Lodi dynasty. The lush greenery and historical monuments make it a perfect spot for a peaceful stroll and a glimpse into Delhi’s history.

Select Citywalk

Select Citywalk is one of the most popular shopping malls in South Delhi. With a wide range of international and Indian brands, fine dining options, and entertainment zones, it’s a perfect place to indulge in some retail therapy.
