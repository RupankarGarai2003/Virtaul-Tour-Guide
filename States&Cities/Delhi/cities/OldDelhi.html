Top Famous Tourist Places in Old Delhi

Old Delhi is renowned for its historical landmarks, rich cultural heritage, and bustling markets, reflecting the charm of ancient India.

Red Fort

The Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an iconic symbol of India's rich history and was the main residence of the Mughal emperors for over 200 years. Built in 1648 by Shah Jahan, this magnificent fort is famous for its massive red sandstone walls and architecture.

Jama Masjid

Jama Masjid, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1656, is one of the largest mosques in India. This grand mosque is known for its striking architecture, beautiful courtyard, and its spiritual significance to the Islamic community.

Chandni Chowk

Chandni Chowk is one of the oldest and busiest markets in Old Delhi. It is known for its narrow lanes filled with shops selling traditional Indian clothes, jewelry, and delicious street food, making it a must-visit destination for tourists.

Raj Ghat

Raj Ghat is the memorial dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi, the Father of the Nation. Located on the banks of the Yamuna River, it is a serene place where visitors can pay their respects and reflect on Gandhi's contributions to India's independence.

Fatehpuri Masjid

Fatehpuri Masjid, built in 1650 by one of Emperor Shah Jahan's wives, is another significant mosque in Old Delhi. Located at the western end of Chandni Chowk, this mosque is known for its simple yet elegant architecture.

Digambar Jain Lal Mandir

Digambar Jain Lal Mandir is the oldest and most famous Jain temple in Delhi. Located opposite the Red Fort, this temple is known for its stunning red sandstone structure and peaceful ambiance, making it a spiritual haven for Jain devotees.
