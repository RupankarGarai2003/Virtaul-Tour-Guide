Top Famous Tourist Places in Amaravati, Andhra Pradesh

Amaravati, the new capital of Andhra Pradesh, is rich in history and culture, boasting several landmarks that reflect its heritage and modern development.

Amaravati Buddhist Stupa

The Amaravati Buddhist Stupa is a significant archaeological site and one of the largest stupas in India. Dating back to the 3rd century BCE, it is renowned for its intricate carvings and sculptures depicting the life of Buddha.

Amaravati Heritage Museum

The Amaravati Heritage Museum showcases the history and culture of Amaravati through various artifacts, sculptures, and exhibits that highlight the region's Buddhist heritage.

The New Capital City (Amaravati)

Amaravati is not only the capital city of Andhra Pradesh but also a symbol of modern development. The city features wide roads, green spaces, and numerous government buildings designed with contemporary architecture.

Undavalli Caves

The Undavalli Caves are a group of rock-cut caves located on a hilltop near Amaravati. They date back to the 4th-5th century CE and are known for their stunning sculptures and the massive reclining statue of Buddha.

Krishna River

The Krishna River is one of the major rivers in India and flows through Amaravati. The river provides picturesque views and is a popular spot for recreational activities such as boating and picnicking.
