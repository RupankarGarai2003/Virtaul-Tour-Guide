Top Famous Tourist Places in Bikaner, Rajasthan

Bikaner, a city steeped in history, offers a rich blend of architectural marvels, royal heritage, and cultural grandeur, making it one of the must-visit cities in Rajasthan.

Jaisalmer Fort

The Junagarh Fort is a massive complex featuring palaces, courtyards, and temples. Built in the 16th century, this unconquered fort is an epitome of Rajasthan’s grandeur and boasts intricate stone carvings and impressive architecture.

Karni Mata Temple

Known as the ‘Temple of Rats,’ Karni Mata Temple is a unique shrine located in Deshnoke, near Bikaner. The temple is dedicated to Karni Mata, an incarnation of Goddess Durga, and is home to thousands of revered rats.

Lalgarh Palace

Lalgarh Palace, built in the early 20th century, is a stunning blend of European and Indian architectural styles. The palace features intricate latticework and is home to a museum that showcases royal memorabilia.

National Research Centre on Camel

The National Research Centre on Camel is a unique institution dedicated to the research and breeding of camels. Visitors can learn about the various breeds of camels and even enjoy camel rides and milk products.

Gajner Palace

Gajner Palace, situated on the banks of the Gajner Lake, is a stunning heritage hotel that was once a hunting lodge for the Maharajas of Bikaner. The palace’s location amidst lush greenery and serene lake views makes it an ideal retreat.

Bhandasar Jain Temple

The Bhandasar Jain Temple is a 15th-century shrine known for its beautiful architecture and intricate frescoes. The temple is dedicated to Lord Sumatinath and is one of the finest examples of Jain temple art in Rajasthan.
